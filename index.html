Climate Change in the US

Uncovering former president Barak Obama’s actions to fight climate change

What is cliamte change?

Climate change refers to the changes of the weather pattern of a particular location or the planet as a whole. This is a result of humanity’s increased use of fossil fuels which releases carbon dioxide and toxic emissions into the atmosphere. These greenhouse gases have negative implications on the Earth as the increase in pollution and heat will ultimately decrease the health of the people and ecosystems.

Due to these potential long-term issues, the necessity to act upon and reduce the rate of climate change has become a significant topic amongst various communities. Influential figures have either expressed their support by addressing these issues or have chosen to remain silent.

President Obama's stance on climate change

The former 44th President of the United States of America from 2009 -2017, Barack Obama has been at the helm of the world’s largest and most influential countries. He believes that no challenge poses a greater threat to our children, our planet, and future generations than climate change — and that no other country on Earth is better equipped to lead the world towards a solution.

President Obama's Actions

Under President Obama’s leadership, the United States has done more to combat climate change than ever before. One of his most notable government initiatives was the Climate Action Plan.

On June 25th, 2013, President Obama announced his Climate Action Plan (CAP) during a speech at Georgetown University. This comprehensive plan is the first of its kind and was developed by the Administration to strategically achieve three overarching goals:
  • Cut domestic carbon pollution
  • Prepare the United States for climate change impacts
  • Lead international efforts to address global climate change.

However, his voice was not only confined to official conferences and announcements he continued the conversation on social media specifically twitter. Following the Climate Action Plan, Obama utilised the hashtag #ActOnClimate , which was his most used hashtag compared to other topics with over 300 tweets.

The utilisation of social media during his presidency allowed him to become one of the most influential government advocates over the concerns of climate change. This allowed Obama to communicate and raise awareness with a wider demographic to convey his message towards the public of the negative implications of climate change as well as his plans to combat these issues. However, comparing Obama’s actions towards climate change with Trumps, we can see a significant difference.
